11. What crime is committed by a public officer who, without legal ground, prohibits or interrupts the holding of a peaceful meeting?

# Answer Key

1.  **a) Treason**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 114 of the Revised Penal Code defines treason as the act of a Filipino citizen levying war against the government or adhering to its enemies, giving them aid or comfort.

2.  **b) Two**
    *   **Explanation:** The "two-witness rule" for treason, as stated in Article 114, requires the testimony of at least two witnesses to the same overt act.

3.  **c) Delaying release**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 126 of the RPC specifically penalizes a public officer who delays the release of a prisoner beyond the period prescribed by law.

4.  **c) Violation of domicile**
    *   **Explanation:** Violation of domicile (Article 128) is a crime against the fundamental laws of the state, as it infringes on the sanctity of the home. The others are crimes against national security or public order.

5.  **b) Violation of domicile**
    *   **Explanation:** This is the precise definition of violation of domicile under Article 128 of the RPC.

6.  **d) Piracy**
    *   **Explanation:** Piracy is a crime against the law of nations, committed on the high seas. The others are crimes against public order.

7.  **b) Sedition**
    *   **Explanation:** Sedition, under Article 139, involves a public and tumultuous uprising to prevent the execution of a law or for other political or social ends.

8.  **d) Sedition**
    *   **Explanation:** This act falls under one of the objects of sedition as defined in Article 139 of the RPC.

9.  **b) A Filipino citizen**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 116 states that any Filipino citizen who has knowledge of a conspiracy against the government and conceals it is liable for misprision of treason.

10. **c) Intellectually siding with the enemy**
    *   **Explanation:** "Adherence to the enemy" in treason refers to an intellectual or emotional favoring of the enemy's cause.

11. **c) Prohibition, interruption, and dissolution of peaceful meetings**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 131 of the RPC directly penalizes the act of a public officer who, without legal ground, prohibits or interrupts a peaceful meeting.

12. **b) Violation of domicile**
    *   **Explanation:** A search of a dwelling without a warrant by a public officer is a violation of domicile under Article 128.

13. **b) Rebellion**
    *   **Explanation:** This is the definition of rebellion or insurrection under Article 134 of the RPC.

14. **b) Public and tumultuous**
    *   **Explanation:** A key element of sedition is that the uprising must be public and tumultuous.

15. **b) Blackmail**
    *   **Explanation:** This act of threatening to publish a libel for compensation is a form of blackmail under Article 357 of the RPC.

16. **a) Seriously intimidating a teacher while in the performance of her duties**
    *   **Explanation:** A teacher is a person in authority, and seriously intimidating them in the performance of their duties constitutes direct assault.

17. **c) Correspondence with hostile country**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 120 of the RPC punishes correspondence with a hostile country during a time of war.

18. **a) Revelation of secrets**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 229 of the RPC penalizes the revelation of secrets by a public officer.

19. **c) Committed by government forces**
    *   **Explanation:** Rebellion is committed by civilians or non-government forces against the government. If committed by government forces, it would be a coup d'etat.

20. **c) Misprision of treason**
    *   **Explanation:** Misprision of treason, under Article 116, is the concealment of a known conspiracy to commit treason.

21. **b) Violation of the right of suffrage**
    *   **Explanation:** Preventing a person from exercising their right to vote is a crime against the fundamental laws of the state, specifically a violation of the right of suffrage.

22. **d) Rebellion**
    *   **Explanation:** Rebellion is a crime against public order. The others are crimes against national security.

23. **d) Illegal possession and use of false treasury or bank notes**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 168 of the RPC penalizes the illegal possession and use of false treasury or bank notes.

24. **c) Coup d'etat**
    *   **Explanation:** This scenario perfectly describes a coup d'etat under Article 134-A of the RPC.

25. **c) Highway robbery/Brigandage**
    *   **Explanation:** Presidential Decree No. 532 defines and penalizes highway robbery/brigandage.

26. **b) A large, noisy crowd**
    *   **Explanation:** A tumult is a disorderly and noisy commotion or disturbance by a crowd.

27. **b) Inciting others to commit rebellion through speeches, writings, etc.**
    *   **Explanation:** Article 138 of the RPC defines inciting to rebellion as inciting others to commit rebellion, without taking up arms or being in open hostility.

28. **a) Direct bribery**
    *   **Explanation:** This is the definition of direct bribery under Article 210 of the RPC.

29. **b) Offending the religious feelings**
    *   **Explanation:** This act is penalized as offending religious feelings under Article 133 of the RPC.

30. **b) Conspiracy to commit sedition**
    *   **Explanation:** This is the definition of conspiracy to commit sedition under Article 141 of the RPC.
