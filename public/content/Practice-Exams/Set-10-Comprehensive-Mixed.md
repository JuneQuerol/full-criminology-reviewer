# Answer Key

**1. c) Sufficient provocation on the part of the person defending himself**
- *Explanation:* The requisites of self-defense are: (1) unlawful aggression, (2) reasonable necessity of the means employed to prevent or repel it, and (3) lack of sufficient provocation on the part of the person defending himself.

**2. c) Accessory**
- *Explanation:* An accessory is one who, having knowledge of the commission of the crime, and without having participated therein, either as principal or accomplice, takes part subsequent to its commission in any of the following manners: (1) By profiting himself or assisting the offender to profit by the effects of the crime; (2) By concealing or destroying the body of the crime, or the effects or instruments thereof, in order to prevent its discovery; or (3) By harboring, concealing, or assisting in the escape of the principal of the crime, provided the accessory acts with abuse of his public functions or whenever the author of the crime is guilty of treason, parricide, murder, or an attempt to take the life of the Chief Executive, or is known to be habitually guilty of some other crime.

**3. d) Extortion**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of extortion under the Revised Penal Code.

**4. c) The tenant is not permitted to deny the title of his landlord at the time of the commencement of the relation of landlord and tenant between them.**
- *Explanation:* This is a conclusive presumption under the Rules of Court. The other options are disputable presumptions.

**5. a) 20 years**
- *Explanation:* Article 92 of the Revised Penal Code provides that crimes punishable by death, reclusion perpetua, or reclusion temporal shall prescribe in twenty years.

**6. d) Proof beyond reasonable doubt**
- *Explanation:* In a criminal case, the accused is entitled to an acquittal, unless his guilt is shown beyond a reasonable doubt.

**7. a) Miranda Rights**
- *Explanation:* These are the rights of a person under custodial investigation, as enunciated in the landmark case of Miranda v. Arizona.

**8. b) Robbery**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of robbery under the Revised Penal Code.

**9. a) R.A. 9165 (Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002)**
- *Explanation:* This is the special law that defines and penalizes the illegal trafficking and possession of dangerous drugs.

**10. b) Homicide**
- *Explanation:* Homicide is the killing of a human being by another, which is not parricide, murder, or infanticide.

**11. b) Arraignment**
- *Explanation:* Arraignment is the formal reading of a criminal charging document in the presence of the defendant to inform them of the charges against them. In response to arraignment, the accused is expected to enter a plea.

**12. c) Hearsay evidence**
- *Explanation:* Hearsay is a statement, other than one made by the declarant while testifying at the trial or hearing, offered in evidence to prove the truth of the matter asserted.

**13. c) Parricide**
- *Explanation:* Parricide is the crime of killing a close relative. The use of poison would make it qualified parricide, but the crime is still parricide.

**14. a) R.A. 9372 (Human Security Act of 2007)**
- *Explanation:* This law was enacted to provide a legal framework for the prevention, prosecution, and punishment of acts of terrorism in the Philippines.

**15. b) Complaint**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of a complaint under the Rules of Criminal Procedure.

**16. c) The original document itself**
- *Explanation:* This is the Best Evidence Rule, which requires the original document to be produced whenever its contents are the subject of inquiry.

**17. c) Rape**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of rape under the Revised Penal Code.

**18. a) R.A. 9208 (Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act of 2003)**
- *Explanation:* This law was enacted to institute policies to eliminate trafficking in persons, especially women and children, and to establish the necessary institutional mechanisms for the protection and support of trafficked persons.

**19. c) State of necessity**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of the justifying circumstance of state of necessity under the Revised Penal Code.

**20. b) Bail**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of bail under the Rules of Criminal Procedure.

**21. d) Personal Knowledge Rule**
- *Explanation:* This rule provides that a witness can testify only to those facts which he knows of his personal knowledge; that is, which are derived from his own perception.

**22. c) Estafa**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of estafa (swindling) under the Revised Penal Code.

**23. b) R.A. 9262**
- *Explanation:* This is the Anti-Violence Against Women and Their Children Act of 2004.

**24. c) Praeter intentionem**
- *Explanation:* This is the Latin term for the mitigating circumstance of "no intention to commit so grave a wrong."

**25. b) Trial**
- *Explanation:* A trial is the formal examination of evidence before a judge, and typically before a jury, in order to decide guilt in a case of criminal or civil proceedings.

**26. a) Relevant evidence**
- *Explanation:* Relevant evidence is evidence that has any tendency to make the existence of any fact that is of consequence to the determination of the action more probable or less probable than it would be without the evidence.

**27. a) Trespass to dwelling**
- *Explanation:* This is the definition of trespass to dwelling under the Revised Penal Code.

**28. d) R.A. 8049**
- *Explanation:* This is the Anti-Hazing Law.

**29. d) For a price, reward, or promise**
- *Explanation:* This is one of the aggravating circumstances under the Revised Penal Code.

**30. d) Judgment**
- *Explanation:* A judgment is the final ruling of a court of competent jurisdiction on the matters submitted to it.
