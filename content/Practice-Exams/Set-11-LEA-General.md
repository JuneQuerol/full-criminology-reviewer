### What is the primary law that established the Philippine National Police (PNP) under a reorganized Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG)?

- [ ] Republic Act No. 8551
- [x] Republic Act No. 6975
- [ ] Republic Act No. 9708
- [ ] Presidential Decree No. 765

> Explanation: Republic Act No. 6975, otherwise known as the "DILG Act of 1990," established the PNP, absorbing the members of the former Philippine Constabulary and the Integrated National Police.

### The PNP is administered and controlled by which government body?

- [x] National Police Commission (NAPOLCOM)
- [ ] Department of National Defense (DND)
- [ ] Civil Service Commission (CSC)
- [ ] Office of the President

> Explanation: The NAPOLCOM is the agency mandated to administer and control the PNP. It has the authority to oversee police operations, personnel, and administration to ensure the police service is efficient, effective, and accountable.

### Under the principle of unity of command, a subordinate should ideally receive orders from how many superiors?

- [ ] Two
- [x] One
- [ ] Three
- [ ] As many as necessary

> Explanation: The principle of unity of command states that each subordinate should report to and receive orders from only one superior. This avoids confusion and conflicting directives.

### Which of the following best describes "span of control" in police administration?

- [ ] The total number of officers in a police station.
- [x] The number of subordinates a superior can effectively supervise.
- [ ] The geographical area covered by a police unit.
- [ ] The duration of a police officer's tour of duty.

> Explanation: Span of control refers to the optimal number of subordinates that a manager or supervisor can effectively lead, guide, and control.

### This refers to the backbone of police operations, providing continuous police presence and visibility to deter crime.

- [ ] Investigation
- [x] Patrol
- [ ] Traffic Management
- [ ] Intelligence

> Explanation: Patrol is considered the backbone of police operations because it is the most visible police function and is responsible for crime prevention, deterrence, and initial response.

### What does the "O" in the P-O-S-D-Co-R-B management acronym stand for?

- [ ] Operations
- [x] Organizing
- [ ] Oversight
- [ ] Orders

> Explanation: POSDCORB is an acronym for Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, Coordinating, Reporting, and Budgeting. Organizing involves creating the structure of authority and communication.

### Republic Act No. 8551 is also known as:

- [ ] The PNP Reorganization Act of 1990
- [x] The PNP Reform and Reorganization Act of 1998
- [ ] The DILG Act
- [ ] The Police Professionalization Act

> Explanation: RA 8551 amended certain provisions of RA 6975 to reform and reorganize the PNP, aiming to strengthen and professionalize the police force.

### What is the compulsory retirement age for PNP members?

- [ ] 60 years old
- [x] 56 years old
- [ ] 65 years old
- [ ] 55 years old

> Explanation: Under existing laws, the compulsory retirement age for uniformed members of the PNP is 56 years old.

### The power of NAPOLCOM to decide administrative cases against PNP members is an exercise of its:

- [ ] Administrative control
- [x] Quasi-judicial power
- [ ] Executive supervision
- [ ] Operational oversight

> Explanation: When NAPOLCOM adjudicates cases, it is exercising its quasi-judicial power, which is the authority to hear and decide on matters brought before it.

### Who has the authority to appoint Police Commissioned Officers (PCO) from the rank of Inspector to Superintendent?

- [x] The President of the Philippines
- [ ] The Chief of the PNP
- [ ] The DILG Secretary
- [ ] The NAPOLCOM Chairperson

> Explanation: The President of the Philippines, upon the recommendation of the Chief PNP and the NAPOLCOM, has the authority to appoint Police Commissioned Officers from Inspector to Superintendent.

### This plan is prepared by police stations to address forseeable emergencies or special events.

- [ ] Management Plan
- [x] Operational Plan
- [ ] Tactical Plan
- [ ] Procedural Plan

> Explanation: Operational plans are developed to give guidance and direction to personnel in the performance of normal police activities or for special events and emergencies.

### Which PNP operational unit is primarily responsible for handling cases of kidnapping and syndicated crimes?

- [ ] Criminal Investigation and Detection Group (CIDG)
- [x] Anti-Kidnapping Group (AKG)
- [ ] Special Action Force (SAF)
- [ ] Highway Patrol Group (HPG)

> Explanation: The PNP Anti-Kidnapping Group (AKG) is the lead unit in the investigation and handling of kidnapping-for-ransom cases and other syndicated crimes.

### The process of attracting and selecting qualified applicants for police service is called:

- [x] Recruitment
- [ ] Training
- [ ] Appointment
- [ ] Placement

> Explanation: Recruitment is the set of activities and processes used to legally obtain a sufficient number of right people at the right time.

### Which of the following is NOT a functional area of police management (POSDCORB)?

- [ ] Planning
- [ ] Staffing
- [x] Scrutinizing
- [ ] Budgeting

> Explanation: The acronym POSDCORB stands for Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, Coordinating, Reporting, and Budgeting. "Scrutinizing" is not part of this classic management model.

### An officer's area of responsibility in a patrol sector is also known as a:

- [ ] Route
- [x] Beat
- [ ] Post
- [ ] Sector

> Explanation: A "beat" is a specific geographical area assigned to a police officer for patrol, either on foot or in a vehicle.

### What is the primary purpose of RA 8551 in relation to RA 6975?

- [ ] To abolish the PNP
- [ ] To merge the PNP with the Armed Forces
- [x] To amend and reform the original PNP law
- [ ] To place the PNP under the DND

> Explanation: RA 8551 was enacted to introduce reforms to the PNP, amending RA 6975 to enhance police professionalism and effectiveness.

### This principle states that an organization is effective if it is structured to achieve its purpose.

- [ ] Principle of Unity of Objective
- [x] Principle of Organizational Efficiency
- [ ] Principle of Scalar Chain
- [ ] Principle of Division of Work

> Explanation: The Principle of Organizational Efficiency dictates that an organization's structure should be designed to achieve its objectives with the minimum possible cost or consequence.

### The head of the National Police Commission (NAPOLCOM) is the:

- [ ] Chief of the PNP
- [x] DILG Secretary as Ex-Officio Chairperson
- [ ] A retired PNP General
- [ ] A senior government official appointed by the President

> Explanation: By law, the Secretary of the Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG) serves as the Ex-Officio Chairperson of the NAPOLCOM.

### Which type of patrol is most effective in high-density urban areas for community engagement?

- [ ] Automobile Patrol
- [x] Foot Patrol
- [ ] Helicopter Patrol
- [ ] Motorcycle Patrol

> Explanation: Foot patrol allows for direct, face-to-face interaction with community members, fostering trust and communication, which is crucial in densely populated urban areas.

### What is the minimum educational qualification for a new applicant to the PNP?

- [ ] High School Graduate
- [ ] College Level
- [x] Baccalaureate Degree
- [ ] Master's Degree

> Explanation: Under RA 8551, all new applicants to the PNP must have a formal baccalaureate degree from a recognized institution of learning.

### The vertical movement of an officer from a lower to a higher position or rank is called:

- [ ] Assignment
- [x] Promotion
- [ ] Attrition
- [ ] Reassignment

> Explanation: Promotion is the upward movement in rank or position of an individual in an organization.

### Which of the following is a "line function" in a police organization?

- [ ] Planning and Research
- [ ] Human Resources
- [x] Patrol and Investigation
- [ ] Budget and Finance

> Explanation: Line functions are the primary, objective-achieving activities of an organization. In policing, this includes patrol, investigation, and traffic control. Staff functions support the line functions.

### This is the systematic and orderly assignment of officers to positions or ranks.

- [x] Placement
- [ ] Staffing
- [ ] Directing
- [ ] Coordinating

> Explanation: Placement is the process of assigning the right person to the right job based on their qualifications and the needs of the organization.

### What is the primary role of the People's Law Enforcement Board (PLEB)?

- [x] To conduct hearings and adjudicate citizen's complaints against uniformed PNP members.
- [ ] To manage the budget of the local police.
- [ ] To appoint the local Chief of Police.
- [ ] To provide legal assistance to police officers.

> Explanation: The PLEB is a disciplinary body at the city/municipal level that has jurisdiction to hear and decide citizen's complaints filed against any member of the PNP.

### A police officer who reports to only one supervisor is following which principle?

- [ ] Span of Control
- [x] Unity of Command
- [ ] Chain of Command
- [ ] Delegation of Authority

> Explanation: Unity of command ensures that there is a clear line of authority and that conflicting orders are avoided by having each employee report to a single supervisor.

### The logical and orderly arrangement of positions in an organization is known as:

- [ ] The organization chart
- [x] The organizational structure
- [ ] The scalar chain
- [ ] The management process

> Explanation: The organizational structure defines how jobs and tasks are divided, grouped, and coordinated.

### Which agency has administrative supervision and control over the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP) and the Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)?

- [ ] Department of Justice (DOJ)
- [x] Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG)
- [ ] Bureau of Corrections (BuCor)
- [ ] National Police Commission (NAPOLCOM)

> Explanation: The DILG, as per RA 6975, exercises administrative supervision and control over the PNP, BJMP, and BFP.

### This plan outlines the procedures to be followed in specific situations, such as bomb threats or hostage crises.

- [ ] Extra-departmental Plan
- [x] Tactical Plan
- [ ] Procedural Plan
- [ ] Management Plan

> Explanation: Tactical plans are developed for specific, non-routine situations that require a pre-determined course of action.

### Under RA 8551, the PNP is considered a community and service-oriented agency, which is fundamentally:

- [ ] Military in character
- [x] Civilian in character
- [ ] A part of the armed forces
- [ ] A national guard

> Explanation: RA 8551 reinforced the civilian character of the PNP, emphasizing its role as a service-oriented agency closer to the community rather than a military force.

### The number of persons one individual can effectively supervise is known as:

- [x] Span of Control
- [ ] Unity of Command
- [ ] Chain of Command
- [ ] Delegation of Authority

> Explanation: Span of control is a key principle of organizational design that deals with the number of subordinates reporting directly to a manager.
