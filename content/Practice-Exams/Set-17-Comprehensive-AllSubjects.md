### (LEA) This law established the Philippine National Police (PNP) under a reorganized DILG, defining its powers, functions, and organizational structure.
- [ ] RA 8551
- [x] RA 6975
- [ ] RA 9262
- [ ] PD 968

> Explanation: Republic Act No. 6975, or the DILG Act of 1990, is the foundational law that created the PNP from the former Philippine Constabulary and Integrated National Police.

### (CLJ) What are the two elements of a felony?
- [ ] Actus reus and Mens rea
- [x] Dolo (deceit) and Culpa (fault)
- [ ] Intent and Motive
- [ ] Act and Omission

> Explanation: Article 3 of the Revised Penal Code defines felonies as acts or omissions punishable by law, committed either with deliberate intent (dolo) or by means of fault (culpa).

### (CRIMINALISTICS) A fingerprint pattern that has two deltas and at least one ridge making a complete circuit is a:
- [ ] Loop
- [x] Whorl
- [ ] Arch
- [ ] Tented Arch

> Explanation: The presence of two deltas is a key characteristic of all whorl-type patterns, distinguishing them from loops (one delta) and arches (no delta).

### (CDI) What is the primary duty of the first officer responding to a crime scene?
- [ ] To immediately apprehend the suspect.
- [x] To preserve life and render first aid.
- [ ] To begin the search for evidence.
- [ ] To notify the media.

> Explanation: The preservation of life is the most critical and immediate priority for any first responder, taking precedence over all other duties.

### (SCE) According to Merton's Strain Theory, crime is a result of the gap between:
- [ ] The rich and the poor.
- [x] Culturally defined goals and the legitimate means to achieve them.
- [ ] An individual's self-control and their desires.
- [ ] The police and the community.

> Explanation: Robert Merton's theory posits that when individuals are unable to achieve societal goals (like wealth) through legitimate means, the resulting strain may lead them to use illegitimate, criminal means.

### (CA) In the Philippine correctional system, a convicted offender sentenced to a prison term of more than three years is sent to a:
- [ ] City or Municipal Jail
- [x] National Prison or Penitentiary managed by BuCor
- [ ] Provincial Jail
- [ ] Police Lock-up

> Explanation: The Bureau of Corrections (BuCor) has jurisdiction over national prisoners, who are those with sentences exceeding three years. Jails (BJMP and Provincial) are for those with shorter sentences or awaiting trial.

### (LEA) The number of subordinates that a superior can effectively supervise is known as:
- [ ] Unity of Command
- [x] Span of Control
- [ ] Delegation of Authority
- [ ] Chain of Command

> Explanation: Span of control is a core management principle that dictates the number of personnel or units one manager can effectively oversee.

### (CRIMINALISTICS) Which instrument is essential for comparing the unique striations on two bullets?
- [ ] Scanning Electron Microscope
- [x] Comparison Microscope
- [ ] Stereomicroscope
- [ ] Gas Chromatograph

> Explanation: The comparison microscope is the cornerstone of firearms identification, allowing an examiner to view two objects simultaneously in a single field of view to find a match.

### (CLJ) Is motive an essential element of a crime?
- [ ] Yes, it is always required for conviction.
- [x] No, it is generally not essential, except in certain crimes where the law makes it an element.
- [ ] Yes, but only for felonies committed with dolo.
- [ ] No, it is only important for determining the penalty.

> Explanation: Motive is the reason or impulse that drives a person to commit an act. While useful for investigation, it is not a required element for conviction in most crimes, as intent is the critical factor.

### (SCE) The "Broken Windows" theory suggests that:
- [ ] Crime is caused by poverty.
- [x] Visible signs of disorder and neglect in a community encourage more serious crime.
- [ ] Police should focus on arresting serious felons only.
- [ ] Vandalism is the root of all crime.

> Explanation: This theory posits that tolerating minor offenses (like vandalism, loitering, and public drinking) creates an environment of disorder that signals that no one is in control, which can lead to an increase in more serious crimes.

### (CDI) The "fruit of the poisonous tree" doctrine states that:
- [ ] All evidence must be properly packaged.
- [x] Evidence obtained as a result of an illegal act by law enforcement is inadmissible in court.
- [ ] The testimony of an informant is always suspect.
- [ ] A confession must be corroborated by other evidence.

> Explanation: This legal doctrine is an extension of the exclusionary rule. If evidence is initially obtained illegally (the "poisonous tree"), any subsequent evidence derived from it (the "fruit") is also tainted and inadmissible.

### (CA) A general pardon granted to a group of persons, often for political offenses, is called:
- [ ] Pardon
- [x] Amnesty
- [ ] Parole
- [ ] Reprieve

> Explanation: Amnesty is an act of sovereign power that grants oblivion or a general pardon for a past offense, usually to a class of persons (e.g., rebels). It requires the concurrence of Congress.

### (CRIMINALISTICS) The chemical reaction of Ninhydrin is used to develop latent fingerprints on what type of surface?
- [ ] Non-porous surfaces like glass or metal.
- [x] Porous surfaces like paper or cardboard.
- [ ] Human skin.
- [ ] Bloody surfaces.

> Explanation: Ninhydrin reacts with the amino acids present in fingerprint sweat, making it particularly effective for developing prints on porous materials like paper.

### (LEA) Who serves as the ex-officio chairperson of the National Police Commission (NAPOLCOM)?
- [ ] The Chief of the PNP.
- [x] The Secretary of the DILG.

### (CLJ) A person who, without having participated in a crime as principal or accomplice, profits by or assists the offender to profit by the effects of the crime, is an:
- [ ] Accomplice
- [x] Accessory
- [ ] Co-principal
- [ ] Conspirator

> Explanation: An accessory is one who has knowledge of the commission of the crime and, subsequent to its commission, takes steps to conceal the crime, the offender, or its effects.

### (CDI) The three primary tools of criminal investigation are Information, Instrumentation, and what else?
- [ ] Intuition
- [x] Interview and Interrogation
- [ ] Informants
- [ ] Integrity

> Explanation: These are the "3 I's" of investigation. Information is the knowledge gathered, Instrumentation is the use of forensic science, and Interview/Interrogation is the method of gathering testimonial evidence.

### (SCE) This theory posits that crime is a result of low self-control combined with the opportunity to commit a crime.
- [ ] Strain Theory
- [x] General Theory of Crime (Gottfredson & Hirschi)
- [ ] Social Disorganization Theory
- [ ] Labeling Theory

> Explanation: Gottfredson and Hirschi's General Theory of Crime argues that low self-control, established in early childhood, is the primary individual-level cause of all forms of criminal and reckless behavior.

- [ ] The Supreme Court
- [x] The Juvenile Justice System

- [ ] Parole
- [x] Probation
- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Commutation

> Explanation: Probation is a judicial function and a privilege granted by the court, allowing a convict to remain in the community instead of being incarcerated.

### (CRIMINALISTICS) What is the primary purpose of using a scale or ruler in a crime scene photograph?
- [ ] To point to the evidence.
- [x] To provide a size reference for the object being photographed.
- [ ] To measure the distance from the camera.
- [ ] To focus the camera lens.

### (LEA) This principle of organization states that a subordinate should receive orders from only one superior.
- [ ] Span of Control
- [x] Unity of Command
- [ ] Chain of Command
- [ ] Division of Work

> Explanation: Unity of Command prevents confusion and conflicting directives by ensuring that each employee reports to a single immediate supervisor.

### (CLJ) A crime that is consummated in a single instant is known as a/an:
- [ ] Continuing crime
- [x] Instantaneous crime
- [ ] Transitory crime
- [ ] Complex crime

> Explanation: An instantaneous crime is one where all the elements for its commission occur in one single moment (e.g., homicide). A continuing crime is one where the criminal act is ongoing (e.g., kidnapping).

### (CDI) A "buy-bust" operation is a form of what?
- [ ] A sting operation
- [x] An entrapment operation
- [ ] A surveillance operation
- [ ] A search and seizure

> Explanation: A buy-bust is a specific type of entrapment where an undercover officer poses as a buyer of illicit goods (usually drugs) to arrest the seller in the act of committing the crime.

### (SCE) A police officer who accepts unsolicited favors or gifts (like free coffee) is referred to as a:
- [x] "Grass-eater"
- [ ] "Meat-eater"
- [ ] "Rogue cop"
- [ ] "Blue-coat"

> Explanation: The Knapp Commission coined this term to describe officers who passively engage in minor corruption, as opposed to "meat-eaters" who actively seek out corrupt opportunities.

### (CA) Sir Walter Crofton's "Irish System" is considered the forerunner of modern:
- [ ] Probation
- [x] Parole
- [ ] Solitary Confinement
- [ ] The prison farm system

> Explanation: The Irish System was a progressive stage system that culminated in a "ticket-of-leave," a conditional release under supervision that is the direct historical antecedent of parole.

### (CRIMINALISTICS) The study of the effects of poisons on the human body is:
- [ ] Serology
- [x] Toxicology
- [ ] Pharmacology
- [ ] Pathology

> Explanation: Forensic toxicology is the specific discipline that deals with the detection and interpretation of drugs and poisons in biological samples for legal purposes.

### (LEA) The backbone of police operations, responsible for continuous police presence and crime prevention, is:
- [ ] Investigation
- [x] Patrol
- [ ] Intelligence
- [ ] Traffic management

> Explanation: The patrol function is the most visible aspect of policing and is considered the core of police operations, tasked with deterrence, observation, and first response.

### (CLJ) The penalty of "reclusion perpetua" is an example of what kind of penalty?
- [ ] Correctional penalty

- [ ] Light penalty
- [ ] Capital penalty

### (CDI) The questioning of a hostile or uncooperative person suspected of a crime is called:
- [ ] Interview
- [x] Interrogation
- [ ] Deposition
- [ ] Inquiry

> Explanation: An interrogation is accusatory in nature and is designed to elicit incriminating statements or a confession from a suspect.

### (CDI) The "corpus delicti" of a crime refers to:
- [ ] The dead body in a homicide case.
- [x] The essential elements or body of the crime.
- [ ] The modus operandi of the suspect.
- [ ] The weapon used in the crime.

> Explanation: Corpus delicti means "body of the crime." It refers to the fundamental facts that prove a crime has actually been committed, such as a dead body (in a homicide) and evidence that the death was criminally caused.

### (SCE) The theory that people commit crimes because they have been negatively labeled by society is called:
- [ ] Strain Theory
- [x] Labeling Theory
- [ ] Control Theory
- [ ] Conflict Theory

> Explanation: Labeling theory focuses on the societal reaction to deviance, arguing that the act of being labeled a "criminal" can lead an individual to internalize that identity and commit more crime.
