### 1. What is the primary purpose of Presidential Decree No. 968, also known as the Adult Probation Law of 1976?

- [ ] To institutionalize convicted offenders for long-term rehabilitation.
- [x] To provide an opportunity for a repentant offender to be rehabilitated without institutional confinement.
- [ ] To establish a system for the automatic parole of all first-time offenders.
- [ ] To replace imprisonment with mandatory community service for all types of crimes.

> Explanation: The fundamental philosophy of P.D. 968 is to provide a second chance to first-time offenders who are likely to respond to individualized, community-based treatment programs, thus promoting their reformation and reintegration into society without the need for imprisonment.

### 2. An application for probation is filed by the accused with which of the following?

- [ ] The Supreme Court
- [ ] The Parole and Probation Administration
- [x] The trial court that convicted the offender
- [ ] The Department of Justice

> Explanation: Section 4 of P.D. 968 explicitly states that the offender must file an application for probation with the trial court which has jurisdiction over the case.

### 3. Which of the following is NOT a mandatory condition of probation?

- [ ] To report to the probation officer designated to supervise him at least once a month.
- [ ] To not commit another offense.
- [x] To abstain from visiting cabarets, beerhouses, and similar establishments.
- [ ] To present himself to the probation officer for supervision.

> Explanation: While abstaining from visiting certain establishments can be imposed as a special or discretionary condition by the court, the mandatory conditions listed under Section 10 of P.D. 968 include presenting oneself to the probation officer and not committing another offense. Reporting frequency is also a standard requirement.

### 4. Who is the head of the Parole and Probation Administration?

- [ ] The Secretary of Justice
- [x] The Administrator
- [ ] The Chairman of the Board of Pardons and Parole
- [ ] The Chief Probation Officer

> Explanation: The Parole and Probation Administration (PPA) is headed by an Administrator, who is responsible for the overall management and administration of the agency. The Administrator is assisted by one or more Deputy Administrators.

### 5. What is the key difference between probation and parole?

- [ ] Probation is granted by the President, while parole is granted by the court.
- [x] Probation is an alternative to imprisonment, while parole is a conditional release from prison.
- [ ] Probation is for serious offenses, while parole is for minor offenses.
- [ ] Probation does not require supervision, while parole does.

> Explanation: Probation is a disposition under which a defendant, after conviction and sentence, is released subject to conditions imposed by the court and to the supervision of a probation officer, without serving time in prison. Parole is the conditional release of a prisoner from a correctional institution after they have served the minimum of their sentence.

### 6. Which agency is responsible for investigating and recommending the grant of parole?

- [ ] The Bureau of Corrections
- [x] The Board of Pardons and Parole (BPP)
- [ ] The Parole and Probation Administration (PPA)
- [ ] The trial court that convicted the offender

> Explanation: The Board of Pardons and Parole (BPP) is the government agency authorized to grant parole, recommend pardon to the President, and oversee the parole and executive clemency system.

### 7. Executive clemency refers to the power of the President to grant reprieves, commutations, and pardons. Which form of clemency totally extinguishes criminal liability?

- [x] Absolute Pardon
- [ ] Commutation of Sentence
- [ ] Reprieve
- [ ] Amnesty

> Explanation: An absolute pardon completely wipes out the conviction and all its consequences, restoring the recipient to full civil and political rights. Amnesty also extinguishes criminal liability, but it is a public act granted to classes of persons, while pardon is a private act for individuals.

### 8. A prisoner's sentence is reduced from life imprisonment to 30 years. What form of executive clemency was applied?

- [ ] Absolute Pardon
- [x] Commutation of Sentence
- [ ] Reprieve
- [ ] Amnesty

> Explanation: Commutation of sentence is the reduction of a sentence to a lighter one. It does not erase the conviction but merely shortens the penalty.

### 9. What does GCTA stand for in the context of correctional administration?

- [ ] General Custodial and Treatment Allowance
- [x] Good Conduct Time Allowance
- [ ] Government Correctional Treatment Authority
- [ ] General Clemency and Time Administration

> Explanation: GCTA, or Good Conduct Time Allowance, is a sentence reduction privilege granted to prisoners who show good behavior and diligence. It is a key component of non-institutional corrections as it can expedite a prisoner's eligibility for parole.

### 10. The Volunteer Probation Aide (VPA) program aims to:

- [ ] Replace professional probation officers with volunteers.
- [x] Generate maximum citizen participation in community-based corrections.
- [ ] Provide legal counsel to probationers.
- [ ] Fund the operations of the Parole and Probation Administration.

> Explanation: The VPA program is a strategy of the PPA to engage citizens in the rehabilitation of offenders. VPAs act as partners to probation officers, providing guidance and support to probationers, thus enhancing the effectiveness of the community corrections program.

### 11. Which of the following offenders is disqualified from being granted probation?

- [ ] Those sentenced to serve a maximum term of imprisonment of not more than six years.
- [x] Those convicted of any crime against national security.
- [ ] Those who have been previously convicted by final judgment of an offense punished by imprisonment of not more than one month and one day.
- [ ] Those who are first-time offenders.

> Explanation: Section 9 of P.D. 968 explicitly lists the disqualifications for applying for probation. Among them are those convicted of crimes against national security or the public order.

### 12. The post-sentence investigation report (PSIR) is prepared by whom?

- [ ] The judge who convicted the offender.
- [x] The probation officer.
- [ ] The prosecutor.
- [ ] The clerk of court.

> Explanation: Upon receipt of the application for probation, the court orders the probation officer to conduct a post-sentence investigation and submit a report (PSIR). This report serves as the basis for the court's decision to grant or deny probation.

### 13. What is the period within which the court must resolve an application for probation from the date of its receipt of the PSIR?

- [ ] 5 days
- [x] 15 days
- [ ] 30 days
- [ ] 60 days

> Explanation: Section 8 of P.D. 968 provides that "The court shall resolve the application for probation not later than fifteen (15) days from the date of its receipt of the investigation report."

### 14. What happens if a probationer violates any of the conditions of probation?

- [ ] The probation is automatically extended for one year.
- [ ] The probationer is required to pay a fine.
- [x] The court may revoke the probation and order the probationer to serve the original sentence.
- [ ] The probationer is given a warning and placed under stricter supervision.

> Explanation: If a violation is established, the court has the discretion to revoke the grant of probation and order the probationer to serve the sentence originally imposed. This is a serious consequence of non-compliance.

### 15. This is a temporary stay of the execution of a sentence.

- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Commutation
- [x] Reprieve
- [ ] Amnesty

> Explanation: A reprieve, granted by the President, is the temporary postponement of the execution of a sentence, especially a death sentence.

### 16. What is the maximum period of probation for an offender sentenced to a term of imprisonment of more than one year?

- [x] Not more than six years.
- [ ] Not more than two years.
- [ ] Equal to the term of imprisonment.
- [ ] Ten years.

> Explanation: Section 14 of P.D. 968 specifies that if the offender is sentenced to imprisonment of more than one year, the probation period shall not exceed six years. If the sentence is one year or less, the period shall not exceed two years.

### 17. The Board of Pardons and Parole is composed of the Secretary of Justice as Chairman and how many members?

- [ ] Four members
- [ ] Five members
- [x] Six members
- [ ] Seven members

> Explanation: The Board of Pardons and Parole is composed of the Secretary of Justice as ex-officio Chairman, and six members appointed by the President. One member must be a sociologist, one a clergyman or educator, one a psychiatrist, and one must be a woman.

### 18. An offender is considered for parole after serving what portion of his sentence?

- [ ] One-half of the maximum sentence.
- [x] The minimum sentence.
- [ ] One-third of the maximum sentence.
- [ ] The full sentence.

> Explanation: A prisoner becomes eligible for parole consideration once he has served the minimum period of his indeterminate sentence. The Board of Pardons and Parole then reviews the case to determine if the prisoner is fit for conditional release.

### 19. Which of the following is NOT a community-based correction program?

- [ ] Probation
- [ ] Parole
- [x] Serving a sentence in a maximum-security prison
- [ ] Community service

> Explanation: Community-based corrections are programs that provide an alternative to traditional incarceration. Probation, parole, and community service all allow the offender to remain in the community under supervision, whereas serving a sentence in a prison is institutional correction.

### 20. The final discharge of a probationer shall operate to restore to him all civil rights lost or suspended as a result of conviction and to:

- [x] Fully discharge his liability for any fine imposed as to the offense for which probation was granted.
- [ ] Make him eligible for any appointive public office.
- [ ] Erase the fact of his conviction from the records.
- [ ] Allow him to run for elective office immediately.

> Explanation: Section 16 of P.D. 968 states that the final discharge of the probationer shall operate to restore all civil rights and fully discharge his liability for any fine. However, it does not erase the record of conviction.

### 21. Which law governs the granting of Good Conduct Time Allowance (GCTA)?

- [ ] Presidential Decree No. 968
- [x] Republic Act No. 10592
- [ ] The Indeterminate Sentence Law (Act No. 4103)
- [ ] Republic Act No. 9165

> Explanation: R.A. 10592 amended articles of the Revised Penal Code to increase the GCTA given to inmates, allowing for a more significant reduction of sentence time for good behavior.

### 22. What is the primary role of a probation officer in the probation system?

- [ ] To arrest violators of the law.
- [x] To investigate, supervise, and rehabilitate probationers.
- [ ] To act as legal counsel for the probationer.
- [ ] To determine the sentence of the convicted offender.

> Explanation: The probation officer's main duties are to conduct a thorough investigation to help the court decide on the probation application and, if granted, to supervise the probationer's activities to ensure compliance with the conditions and to facilitate their rehabilitation.

### 23. This is a special form of pardon exercised by the President of the Philippines that is granted to a class of persons who have committed political offenses.

- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Reprieve
- [x] Amnesty
- [ ] Commutation of Sentence

> Explanation: Amnesty is a sovereign act of oblivion for past acts, granted by a government to a group of persons who are guilty of (usually political) offenses. It is different from pardon, which is granted to an individual.

### 24. A probationer is allowed to travel outside the jurisdiction of the supervising probation office. Who must grant the permission?

- [x] The trial court that granted probation.
- [ ] The Parole and Probation Administrator.
- [ ] The supervising probation officer.
- [ ] The Board of Pardons and Parole.

> Explanation: One of the conditions of probation is not to change residence without the prior permission of the court. Any travel outside the court's jurisdiction requires its approval, usually upon the recommendation of the supervising probation officer.

### 25. The Indeterminate Sentence Law (ISL) is intended to:

- [ ] Provide for a fixed and non-negotiable prison term.
- [ ] Abolish the concept of parole.
- [x] Uplift and redeem valuable human material and prevent unnecessary and excessive deprivation of liberty.
- [ ] Ensure that all convicted felons serve their maximum sentence.

> Explanation: The purpose of the ISL (Act No. 4103) is to promote the reformation of offenders by allowing for their conditional release on parole after serving a minimum sentence, thus encouraging good behavior and rehabilitation.

### 26. In case of a probationer's death before the termination of probation, what happens to his criminal liability?

- [ ] His heirs will assume the liability.
- [ ] The liability is transferred to the state.
- [x] Both criminal and civil liability arising from the crime are extinguished.
- [ ] Only the criminal liability is extinguished, but the civil liability subsists.

> Explanation: As a general rule under criminal law, the death of the convict extinguishes both criminal liability and any civil liability that is based solely on the delict or crime.

### 27. The power to grant pardon is a non-delegable power of the President. What is its major limitation?

- [ ] It cannot be granted for violations of local ordinances.
- [x] It cannot be granted in cases of impeachment.
- [ ] It cannot be granted for civil contempt.
- [ ] It cannot be granted to foreign nationals.

> Explanation: The Philippine Constitution explicitly states that the President's power to grant pardons and reprieves does not extend to cases of impeachment.

### 28. What is the status of a person under parole?

- [ ] He is a free man with all rights and privileges restored.
- [x] He remains in the legal custody of the state and is under supervision.
- [ ] He is considered to have served his full sentence.
- [ ] He is on temporary leave from prison.

> Explanation: A parolee is not a free individual. They are still serving their sentence, but outside the institution. They are under the legal custody of the state and are supervised by a parole officer. Violation of parole conditions can lead to re-incarceration.

### 29. The purpose of the Post-Sentence Investigation Report (PSIR) is to provide the court with information on the offender's:

- [ ] Guilt or innocence.
- [ ] Financial capacity to post bail.
- [x] Character, antecedents, environment, and mental and physical condition.
- [ ] Political affiliations.

> Explanation: The PSIR is a comprehensive social investigation that helps the court determine if the offender is a suitable candidate for probation. It assesses the offender's personality, background, and the risk they might pose to the community.

### 30. A revocation hearing for probation is summary in nature. What does this mean?

- [ ] The probationer is not allowed to present evidence.
- [ ] The hearing is conducted without a judge.
- [x] The process is swift and does not require the full procedural rules of a criminal trial.
- [ ] A jury decides the outcome of the hearing.

> Explanation: A summary hearing for probation revocation means it is a less formal proceeding compared to a full-blown trial. While the probationer has the right to be heard and present evidence, the rules of evidence and procedure are relaxed to expedite the process.
