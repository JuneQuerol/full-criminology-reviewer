### 1. Under RA 10575, the Bureau of Corrections (BuCor) is principally tasked with what?

- [ ] The administration and supervision of all city and municipal jails.
- [x] The custody and rehabilitation of national offenders, who are sentenced to more than three years of imprisonment.
- [ ] The implementation of the probation and parole system for released inmates.
- [ ] The arrest and detention of individuals accused of committing heinous crimes.

> Explanation: RA 10575, the BuCor Modernization Act, mandates the agency to focus on the custody, security, and rehabilitation of national inmates or those sentenced to an imprisonment term of more than three (3) years. City and municipal jails are managed by the BJMP.

### 2. Which of the following best describes the primary difference between a jail and a prison in the Philippine context?

- [ ] Jails are for females, while prisons are for males.
- [x] Jails are for detainees awaiting trial and offenders with short sentences, while prisons are for convicted offenders with long-term sentences.
- [ ] Jails are managed by the national government, while prisons are managed by local government units.
- [ ] Jails house minimum-security offenders, while prisons house maximum-security offenders.

> Explanation: Jails, managed by the BJMP, house persons awaiting trial (detainees) and those sentenced to imprisonment of not more than three years. Prisons, managed by BuCor, house convicted criminals sentenced to serve imprisonment of more than three years.

### 3. A person arrested for a crime and held for trial is classified as what type of prisoner?

- [ ] Sentenced Prisoner
- [x] Detention Prisoner
- [ ] National Prisoner
- [ ] Municipal Prisoner

> Explanation: A detention prisoner, also known as a detainee, is an individual held in custody who is awaiting trial or a verdict. A sentenced prisoner is one whose guilt has been established and is serving a sentence.

### 4. What is the main purpose of the Reception and Diagnostic Center (RDC) in the BuCor system?

- [ ] To immediately assign the inmate to a permanent prison facility.
- [ ] To conduct the execution of the sentence.
- [x] To diagnose, classify, and determine the appropriate security and rehabilitation program for an inmate.
- [ ] To serve as the primary holding facility for all newly-arrested national offenders.

> Explanation: The RDC is the initial point of contact for a newly committed national prisoner. It is where the inmate undergoes a series of examinations (medical, psychological, sociological) for about 60 days to properly classify them and create a tailored treatment plan before they are transferred to a specific prison facility.

### 5. An inmate sentenced to death or a term of 20 years to life imprisonment would be classified under which security level?

- [ ] Minimum Security
- [ ] Medium Security
- [x] Maximum Security
- [ ] Super Maximum Security

> Explanation: Maximum security inmates include those sentenced to death (now reclusion perpetua), those with a sentence of 20 years or more, those whose sentence is under review by the Supreme Court, and those with a history of escape or who are considered highly dangerous.

### 6. Which of the following is NOT considered a primary institutional treatment program?

- [ ] Educational and Vocational Training
- [ ] Religious and Spiritual Services
- [x] Conjugal Visits
- [ ] Work and Livelihood Programs

> Explanation: While conjugal visits are a privilege that may be granted, they are not a core institutional treatment or rehabilitation program. The primary treatment programs are focused on reform through education, work, and spiritual/moral guidance.

### 7. RA 10575 aims to modernize BuCor. Which of the following is a key provision of this act?

- [ ] Abolition of the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP).
- [ ] Privatization of all prison facilities.
- [x] Upgrading of facilities, professionalization of staff, and increasing the salary of personnel.
- [ ] Transferring the control of BuCor from the Department of Justice to the Department of the Interior and Local Government.

> Explanation: The BuCor Modernization Act of 2013 (RA 10575) focuses on strengthening the agency by upgrading its facilities, standardizing the recruitment and training of its personnel (professionalization), and raising their salaries and benefits to be at par with their BJMP counterparts. It remains under the DOJ.

### 8. Which government agency manages provincial jails?

- [ ] Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP)
- [x] Provincial Government
- [ ] Bureau of Corrections (BuCor)
- [ ] Department of Justice (DOJ)

> Explanation: While the BJMP supervises city and municipal jails, provincial jails are managed and funded by their respective provincial governments. This is a unique administrative distinction in the Philippine correctional system.

### 9. A prison design where cell blocks extend from a central administrative hub like spokes on a wheel is known as what?

- [ ] Courtyard Style
- [ ] Campus Style
- [x] Radial Design
- [ ] Telephone Pole Design

> Explanation: The radial design, first seen in the Eastern State Penitentiary, features a central control point with wings or cellblocks radiating outwards. This allows for better surveillance and control from a single vantage point.

### 10. The right to be presumed innocent until proven guilty is a fundamental right of a prisoner. In which context is this most applicable?

- [ ] A sentenced prisoner serving his final year.
- [x] A detention prisoner undergoing trial.
- [ ] A parolee under supervision.
- [ ] An inmate in a maximum-security facility.

> Explanation: The presumption of innocence is a constitutional right afforded to an accused person during their trial. Therefore, it is most directly applicable to a detention prisoner who has not yet been convicted. Once convicted, the person is a "sentenced prisoner," and the presumption has been overcome.

### 11. Which BuCor facility is specifically designated as the main penal colony for women?

- [ ] San Ramon Prison and Penal Farm
- [ ] Iwahig Prison and Penal Farm
- [x] Correctional Institution for Women (CIW)
- [ ] New Bilibid Prison

> Explanation: The Correctional Institution for Women (CIW) in Mandaluyong City is the principal prison facility dedicated to the custody and rehabilitation of female national offenders in the Philippines.

### 12. The act of commuting a sentence, such as reducing a life sentence to a fixed number of years, is an act of what?

- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Amnesty
- [x] Executive Clemency
- [ ] Parole

> Explanation: Executive Clemency refers to the powers of the President to grant reprieves, commutations, and pardons. Commutation is the reduction of a sentence to a lighter one. Parole is a conditional release, not a reduction of the sentence itself.

### 13. Which of the following is a key function of the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP)?

- [ ] Formulating policies for the administration of the entire correctional system.
- [x] Providing custody, supervision, and rehabilitation for detainees and short-term prisoners.
- [ ] Administering the parole and probation system.
- [ ] Operating penal colonies for agricultural production.

> Explanation: The BJMP, created under RA 6975, is primarily responsible for the supervision and control of all city, municipal, and district jails nationwide, focusing on individuals awaiting trial or serving sentences of three years or less.

### 14. An inmate who is allowed to work outside the prison facility with minimal supervision is likely classified under what security level?

- [x] Minimum Security
- [ ] Medium Security
- [ ] Maximum Security
- [ ] Detention Prisoner

> Explanation: Minimum security inmates are those who can be reasonably trusted to serve their sentences under open conditions with minimal supervision. They are often assigned to work programs outside the main prison compound, such as in agricultural penal farms.

### 15. The "Good Conduct Time Allowance" (GCTA) under RA 10592 is a privilege that does what?

- [ ] Grants immediate release upon demonstration of good behavior.
- [ ] Provides financial compensation for good conduct.
- [x] Reduces the actual time an inmate serves his/her sentence based on good behavior.
- [ ] Allows an inmate to choose their preferred penal facility.

> Explanation: GCTA is a sentence reduction privilege. It allows for the deduction of a certain number of days from an inmate's sentence for each month of good behavior and obedience to prison rules.

### 16. What type of prison architecture is characterized by a long central corridor with cell blocks or buildings extending from it, resembling a telephone pole?

- [ ] Radial Design
- [x] Telephone Pole Design
- [ ] Courtyard Design
- [ ] Campus Design

> Explanation: The telephone pole design features a long central corridor (the "pole") that serves as the main artery for movement. Extending from this corridor are the functional units like housing, workshops, and dining halls (the "cross-arms"). This design is common in modern maximum-security prisons for its ability to segregate different inmate populations.

### 17. The primary purpose of classifying prisoners is to:

- [ ] Punish them according to the severity of their crime.
- [x] Separate different types of inmates and provide appropriate treatment and security.
- [ ] Determine their eligibility for parole.
- [ ] Assign them to specific work details.

> Explanation: Classification is a cornerstone of modern corrections. Its main goal is to segregate inmates based on their security risk, custody needs, and amenability to treatment to prevent contamination, ensure safety, and facilitate individualized rehabilitation.

### 18. Which right is NOT typically lost upon conviction and imprisonment?

- [ ] The right to vote.
- [ ] The right to hold public office.
- [x] The right to be secure in one's person against unreasonable searches by prison staff.
- [ ] The right to freely travel.

> Explanation: While prisoners have a reduced expectation of privacy, they do not forfeit all constitutional protections. The right against unreasonable searches is modified, but not entirely eliminated, as searches must be related to legitimate penological interests. However, rights like voting (unless restored), holding public office, and freedom of movement are generally forfeited during incarceration.

### 19. A formal process where an inmate is accused of a rule violation, given a notice of charges, and has a chance to present their side before a disciplinary board is known as:

- [ ] Arraignment
- [x] Disciplinary Hearing
- [ ] Classification Board Review
- [ ] Grievance Procedure

> Explanation: A disciplinary hearing is an administrative proceeding within the correctional facility to determine if an inmate has violated institutional rules. It ensures due process by providing notice and an opportunity to be heard before sanctions are imposed.

### 20. The BuCor Modernization Act (RA 10575) established a ranking system for its uniformed personnel similar to that of the:

- [ ] Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP)
- [ ] Philippine Coast Guard (PCG)
- [x] Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP) and Philippine National Police (PNP)
- [ ] Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)

> Explanation: A key aspect of the professionalization under RA 10575 was the standardization of the BuCor's ranking system, modeling it after the systems used by the BJMP, BFP, and PNP to create parity and a clear career path. The ranks now range from Corrections Officer 1 to Corrections Director General.

### 21. What distinguishes a medium-security prisoner from a maximum-security prisoner?

- [ ] Medium-security prisoners are all detainees, while maximum-security prisoners are all sentenced.
- [x] Medium-security prisoners have fewer restrictions and are generally not considered as high-risk or escape-prone as maximum-security prisoners.
- [ ] Medium-security prisoners are housed in dormitories, while maximum-security prisoners are in individual cells.
- [ ] Medium-security prisoners serve sentences of less than 20 years, while maximum-security prisoners serve life sentences only.

> Explanation: The primary distinction is the level of risk and custody required. Medium-security inmates (those serving sentences below 20 years) require fewer security precautions compared to maximum-security inmates (those serving 20 years or more, or considered high-risk), who are housed under more controlled and restrictive conditions.

### 22. Therapeutic Community Modality is a self-help treatment program that primarily addresses:

- [ ] Vocational and technical skills.
- [x] Behavioral and attitudinal problems, particularly related to substance abuse.
- [ ] Physical fitness and health.
- [ ] Religious and spiritual development.

> Explanation: The Therapeutic Community (TC) is a widely used psychosocial treatment model in Philippine corrections (both BuCor and BJMP). It focuses on changing negative patterns of thought, feeling, and behavior through peer support, group therapy, and a structured, hierarchical community environment. It is especially effective for inmates with a history of drug abuse.

### 23. The Iwahig Prison and Penal Farm in Palawan is a notable example of what kind of correctional facility?

- [ ] A super maximum-security facility.
- [ ] A reception and diagnostic center.
- [x] An open penal colony.
- [ ] A metropolitan jail.

> Explanation: Iwahig is a classic example of an "open" penal institution, where inmates of minimum security status are allowed to live with their families in a farm-based community and work on various agricultural projects with minimal supervision.

### 24. Which of the following actions constitutes a violation of a prisoner's basic rights?

- [ ] Requiring inmates to wear uniforms.
- [ ] Censoring an inmate's outgoing mail for security reasons.
- [x] Inflicting corporal punishment for a disciplinary infraction.
- [ ] Searching an inmate's cell without a warrant.

> Explanation: Both Philippine law and international standards (like the UN Nelson Mandela Rules) strictly prohibit the use of corporal punishment, torture, or any cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment as a form of discipline. The other actions are generally accepted as legitimate penological practices.

### 25. Under the rules, who has the authority to authorize the transfer of a national prisoner from one penal facility to another?

- [ ] The Secretary of Justice
- [ ] The Warden of the facility
- [x] The Director General of the Bureau of Corrections
- [ ] The President of the Philippines

> Explanation: The Director General of BuCor has the administrative authority and responsibility for the custody and control of all national prisoners, which includes the power to order their transfer between the different prisons and penal farms under the Bureau's jurisdiction.

### 26. Which of the following is an example of a prison rule designed to maintain good order and discipline?

- [ ] Mandatory participation in religious services.
- [x] Prohibiting contraband such as weapons, drugs, and unauthorized communication devices.
- [ ] Denying access to legal counsel.
- [ ] Forbidding inmates from speaking their native dialect.

> Explanation: The control of contraband is fundamental to maintaining safety, security, and order within a correctional facility. It prevents violence, illegal activities, and escapes. The other options are either illegal (denying counsel) or unreasonable violations of rights.

### 27. The concept of "prison architecture" is primarily concerned with:

- [ ] The aesthetic appeal of the facility.
- [ ] Using the most expensive materials for construction.
- [x] Designing a physical environment that supports security, control, and rehabilitation.
- [ ] Building prisons as far away from populated areas as possible.

> Explanation: Modern prison architecture is a specialized field that balances the need for security (e.g., clear lines of sight, controlled movement, secure perimeters) with the requirements for humane treatment and effective rehabilitation programs (e.g., adequate living space, program areas, natural light).

### 28. Which prisoner classification is given to those who are deemed to be one of the least dangerous and can be trusted in open conditions?

- [ ] Insular Prisoner
- [x] Colonist
- [ ] Maximum Security Prisoner
- [ ] Provincial Prisoner

> Explanation: Within the BuCor system, an inmate who has shown exemplary conduct and has served a portion of their sentence may be classified as a "colonist." This is a high-trust status, granting special privileges, and is typically a prerequisite for being assigned to an open penal farm like Iwahig or Davao.

### 29. The BJMP operates under which executive department of the Philippine government?

- [ ] Department of Justice (DOJ)
- [x] Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG)
- [ ] Office of the President
- [ ] Department of National Defense (DND)

> Explanation: The BJMP is one of the tri-bureaus of the DILG, alongside the Philippine National Police (PNP) and the Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP). The BuCor, on the other hand, is under the DOJ.

### 30. A key principle of the BuCor Modernization Act (RA 10575) is to transform the agency from being purely punitive to being more:

- [ ] Punitive and retributive.
- [x] Scientific and rehabilitative.
- [ ] Private and profit-oriented.
- [ ] Military and security-focused.

> Explanation: A central theme of RA 10575 is the shift in philosophy towards a more modern, scientific, and rehabilitative approach to corrections. This involves using evidence-based programs, professionalizing staff, and focusing on the successful reintegration of offenders into society.
