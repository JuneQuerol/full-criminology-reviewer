### Answer Key

**1. B) Malversation of Public Funds**
*   **Explanation:** Malversation (Article 217, RPC) is committed by any public officer who, by reason of the duties of his office, is accountable for public funds or property, and shall appropriate the same, or shall take or misappropriate or shall consent, or through abandonment or negligence, shall permit any other person to take such public funds or property.

**2. A) Direct Bribery**
*   **Explanation:** Direct Bribery (Article 210, RPC) is committed by a public officer who agrees to perform an act constituting a crime, in connection with the performance of his official duties, in consideration of any offer, promise, gift or present. Here, refraining from making a lawful arrest is a criminal omission.

**3. B) Knowingly Rendering Unjust Judgment**
*   **Explanation:** This is the specific crime under Article 204 of the RPC. Malfeasance is a broader category of wrongful conduct.

**4. B) Nonfeasance**
*   **Explanation:** Nonfeasance is the willful failure to perform a specific duty required by law. It is a form of dereliction of duty under Article 208 of the RPC.

**5. C) Indirect Bribery**
*   **Explanation:** Indirect Bribery (Article 211, RPC) is committed by a public officer who accepts a gift offered to him by reason of his office. There is no need for an agreement to do or not to do an act. The acceptance of the gift itself, by reason of the office, constitutes the crime.

**6. B) Malversation**
*   **Explanation:** Under Article 217 of the RPC, the failure of a public officer to have duly forthcoming any public funds or property with which he is chargeable, upon demand by any duly authorized officer, shall be prima facie evidence that he has put such missing funds or property to personal uses.

**7. B) Conniving with or Consenting to Evasion**
*   **Explanation:** This crime, under Article 223 of the RPC, is committed by a public officer who consents to the escape of a prisoner in his custody or charge. This is a form of infidelity in the custody of prisoners.

**8. C) Revealing Secrets with Abuse of Office**
*   **Explanation:** Article 229 of the RPC penalizes a public officer who reveals any secret of a private individual which he shall have been entrusted with by reason of his office.

**9. C) Security of Communications**
*   **Explanation:** The crime is Violation of Correspondence (Article 280, RPC), which falls under the chapter "Crimes Against the Security of Communications."

**10. A) Fraud against the public treasury**
*   **Explanation:** This act falls under Article 213 of the RPC (Frauds against the public treasury and similar offenses), which includes causing damage to the government through deceit or any other fraudulent act.

**11. B) Libel**
*   **Explanation:** This is the legal definition of Libel under Article 353 of the RPC. While slander is also defamation, libel refers to defamation in written or similar permanent form.

**12. D) Either B or C, depending on the gravity and nature of the imputation**
*   **Explanation:** Oral defamation is punished as either Grave Oral Defamation or Slight Oral Defamation (Slander) under Article 358 of the RPC, depending on the seriousness and offensiveness of the words uttered.

**13. B) Slight Oral Defamation**
*   **Explanation:** When the defamatory words are uttered in the heat of anger or excitement and without serious intent to malign, the crime is generally considered as slight oral defamation.

**14. B) Slander by Deed**
*   **Explanation:** Slander by Deed (Article 359, RPC) is an act performed which is calculated to cast dishonor, discredit, or contempt upon another person. It is defamation through actions.

**15. C) Libel**
*   **Explanation:** The defamatory statement is made through writing (a newspaper column) and made public, which are the essential elements of libel.

**16. C) It must be made public.**
*   **Explanation:** Publicity or publication is a core element of libel. The defamatory statement must be communicated to a third person.

**17. C) Intriguing Against Honor**
*   **Explanation:** Article 364 of the RPC defines this crime as any intrigue which has for its principal purpose to blemish the honor or reputation of a person. It usually involves spreading "gossip" or "tales."

**18. B) Cyberlibel**
*   **Explanation:** Under Republic Act No. 10175 (Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012), libel committed through a computer system or any other similar means which may be devised in the future is prosecuted as Cyberlibel.

**19. A) Privileged communication**
*   **Explanation:** Statements made in the course of judicial, parliamentary, or other official proceedings are generally considered absolutely privileged communication and are not actionable as libel, provided they are relevant to the case.

**20. B) Slander by Deed**
*   **Explanation:** Slapping someone to humiliate them, rather than to cause physical injury, is a classic example of Slander by Deed, as the primary intent is to dishonor the person in public.

**21. B) Adultery**
*   **Explanation:** Adultery (Article 333, RPC) is committed by a married woman who shall have sexual intercourse with a man not her husband and by the man who has carnal knowledge of her, knowing her to be married.

**22. C) Concubinage**
*   **Explanation:** Concubinage (Article 334, RPC) is committed by a husband who shall (1) keep a mistress in the conjugal dwelling, or (2) have sexual intercourse, under scandalous circumstances, with a woman who is not his wife, or (3) cohabit with her in any other place.

**23. B) The offended spouse**
*   **Explanation:** Adultery and Concubinage are private crimes and cannot be prosecuted except upon a complaint filed by the offended spouse (Article 344, RPC).

**24. C) Rape**
*   **Explanation:** This is the definition of Rape under Article 266-A of the RPC.

**25. B) Simple Seduction**
*   **Explanation:** Simple Seduction (Article 338, RPC) involves the use of deceit (enga√±o) to have carnal knowledge of a virgin over 12 and under 18 years of age.

**26. A) Forcible Abduction**
*   **Explanation:** Forcible Abduction (Article 342, RPC) is the abduction of any woman against her will and with lewd designs.

**27. C) Rape**
*   **Explanation:** Acts of Lasciviousness (Article 336, RPC) are committed under the same circumstances as rape (force, intimidation, victim is deprived of reason, etc.), but without the element of carnal knowledge.

**28. B) Qualified Seduction**
*   **Explanation:** Qualified Seduction (Article 337, RPC) is committed when the seduction is perpetrated by a person in a position of authority, influence, or trust over the victim, such as an ascendant, guardian, or teacher. The use of "abuse of confidence" replaces the element of "deceit."

**29. C) Both the husband and his mistress**
*   **Explanation:** The law holds both the unfaithful husband and his concubine criminally liable. The concubine must have knowledge that the man is married.

**30. C) Concubinage**
*   **Explanation:** By its definition in the RPC, only a husband can commit the crime of concubinage. Adultery is committed by the wife (and her paramour), while rape and acts of lasciviousness can be committed by a person of any gender (especially under modern interpretations and special laws).
