## Answer Key

### RA 9165 - Dangerous Drugs Act
1.  **B - Dangerous Drugs Board (DDB)** - *Explanation:* Section 77 of RA 9165 states that the DDB is the policy-making and strategy-formulating body on drug prevention and control.
2.  **C - Drug Paraphernalia** - *Explanation:* This is the term used in Section 3(v) of the Act.
3.  **C - Life imprisonment to death and a fine...** - *Explanation:* Section 11, Possession of Dangerous Drugs, specifies this penalty for possessing 10 grams or more of shabu.
4.  **D - Deliver** - *Explanation:* Section 3(k) defines "deliver" as the act of knowingly passing a dangerous drug to another.
5.  **A - A minimum of 6 months rehabilitation...** - *Explanation:* Section 15, Use of Dangerous Drugs, mandates rehabilitation for a first-time offense.
6.  **B - Reclusion perpetua to death and a fine** - *Explanation:* Section 29, Planting of Evidence, imposes the maximum penalty for this act.
7.  **C - Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA)** - *Explanation:* Section 82 establishes the PDEA as the implementing arm of the DDB.
8.  **D - All of the above.** - *Explanation:* Section 3(t) provides a broad definition of a drug den, covering various activities related to dangerous drugs.
9.  **C - The maximum penalty prescribed for the offense** - *Explanation:* Section 16, Cultivation or Culture of Plants Classified as Dangerous Drugs, states that financiers and organizers shall suffer the maximum penalty.
10. **C - Both A and B** - *Explanation:* Section 27, Criminal Liability of a Public Officer or Employee, provides for both imprisonment and perpetual disqualification.

### RA 9514 - Fire Code
11. **A - Presidential Decree No. 1185** - *Explanation:* Section 2 of RA 9514 explicitly repeals PD 1185.
12. **B - Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)** - *Explanation:* The BFP is designated as the primary agency to enforce the Fire Code.
13. **B - Abatement** - *Explanation:* Section 3 of the Code defines abatement as the act of removing or neutralizing a fire hazard.
14. **D - All of the above** - *Explanation:* A Fire Safety Inspection Certificate is a prerequisite for various permits related to buildings and businesses to ensure compliance with fire safety standards.
15. **A - 5 gallons** - *Explanation:* The IRR of the Fire Code sets limits on the storage of flammable and combustible liquids.
16. **C - Open Burning** - *Explanation:* This is a common term for burning waste and other materials in an open area, which is regulated under the Fire Code.
17. **C - A fine of not more than Php 500,000 and/or imprisonment...** - *Explanation:* Section 11 penalizes obstruction of BFP personnel during operations.
18. **A - Fire Alarm** - *Explanation:* This is the standard term for a device that signals a fire emergency.
19. **A - A fine of not less than Php 100,000** - *Explanation:* Section 13 specifies the penalties for failure to comply with the fire safety requirements of the code.
20. **D - All of the above** - *Explanation:* A fire trap is defined by multiple deficiencies that make a building hazardous during a fire.

### RA 10591 - Firearms and Ammunition
21. **C - Philippine National Police (PNP) through the Firearms and Explosives Office (FEO)** - *Explanation:* Section 4 of RA 10591 designates the PNP-FEO as the sole authority for firearm licensing and registration.
22. **B - Every two (2) years** - *Explanation:* Section 7(j) specifies a renewal period of two years for the license to own and possess.
23. **B - 50 rounds** - *Explanation:* Section 11 of the Act allows a licensed citizen to possess up to 50 rounds of ammunition per registered firearm.
24. **B - Unlawful Carrying of Firearms** - *Explanation:* This is the specific offense of carrying a firearm outside a residence without a Permit to Carry.
25. **D - All of the above** - *Explanation:* The IRR of RA 10591 classifies these weapon types as small arms.
26. **D - Both A and C** - *Explanation:* Section 28 penalizes possession of a firearm with an expired license with a fine and confiscation.
27. **B - Loose firearm** - *Explanation:* Section 3(v) defines a "loose firearm" as an unregistered firearm or one in the possession of an unauthorized individual.
28. **A - Illegal Sale of Firearms** - *Explanation:* This is the offense committed when a person sells a firearm without the proper authority.
29. **A - The Chief of the PNP** - *Explanation:* The Chief of the PNP is the only official authorized to issue a Permit to Carry a Firearm Outside of Residence.
30. **C - Prision mayor** - *Explanation:* Section 34, Planting of Evidence, provides this penalty for planting a firearm.
