### Which government agency in the Philippines is responsible for the supervision of parolees, pardonees, and probationers?

- [ ] Bureau of Corrections (BuCor)
- [ ] Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP)
- [x] Parole and Probation Administration (PPA)
- [ ] Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD)

> Explanation: The PPA is the agency under the Department of Justice (DOJ) tasked with the administration of the parole and probation system in the Philippines.

### The Bureau of Corrections (BuCor) is under the administration of which department?

- [x] Department of Justice (DOJ)
- [ ] Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG)
- [ ] Office of the President
- [ ] Department of National Defense (DND)

> Explanation: The Bureau of Corrections (BuCor) is a line agency of the Department of Justice (DOJ) responsible for the custody and rehabilitation of national offenders sentenced to serve a term of imprisonment of more than three years.

### What is the primary difference between a jail and a prison in the Philippine context?

- [ ] Jails are for male offenders, while prisons are for female offenders.
- [x] Jails house inmates awaiting trial or serving short sentences (up to 3 years), while prisons house convicted offenders serving long-term sentences (over 3 years).
- [ ] Jails are managed by the national government, while prisons are managed by local governments.
- [ ] There is no difference; the terms are interchangeable.

> Explanation: In the Philippines, jails (managed by the BJMP for city/municipal/district facilities) are for pre-trial detainees and those serving sentences of 3 years or less. Prisons (managed by BuCor) are for finally convicted inmates serving sentences exceeding 3 years.

### The Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP) is under the administration of which department?

- [ ] Department of Justice (DOJ)
- [x] Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG)
- [ ] Office of the President
- [ ] National Police Commission (NAPOLCOM)

> Explanation: The BJMP is a bureau under the DILG, responsible for the administration and operation of all city, municipal, and district jails nationwide.

### This is a disposition under which a defendant, after conviction and sentence, is released subject to conditions imposed by the court and to the supervision of a probation officer.

- [x] Probation
- [ ] Parole
- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Amnesty

> Explanation: Probation is a privilege granted by the court to a person convicted of a criminal offense to remain in the community instead of actually going to prison/jail. It is not a right but a discretionary grant.

### This refers to the conditional release of a prisoner from a correctional institution after they have served the minimum period of their prison sentence.

- [ ] Probation
- [x] Parole
- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Commutation of Sentence

> Explanation: Parole is granted by the Board of Pardons and Parole. It is an administrative function, not a judicial one, and it allows an inmate to serve the remainder of their sentence in the community under supervision.

### Who has the exclusive power to grant executive clemency, such as pardon and parole?

- [ ] The Supreme Court
- [ ] The Secretary of Justice
- [x] The President of the Philippines
- [ ] The Director of the Bureau of Corrections

> Explanation: The power to grant executive clemency (pardon, amnesty, reprieve, commutation) is vested in the President of the Philippines under the Constitution. The Board of Pardons and Parole recommends parolees to the President.

### What is the primary goal of non-institutional or community-based corrections?

- [ ] To punish the offender in front of their community.
- [x] To provide a less costly alternative to imprisonment and facilitate the offender's reintegration into society.
- [ ] To eliminate the need for jails and prisons entirely.
- [ ] To allow offenders to serve their sentences at home without any restrictions.

> Explanation: Community-based corrections, like probation and parole, aim to rehabilitate offenders within the community, reduce prison overcrowding, save government resources, and prevent the negative effects of institutionalization.

### The New Bilibid Prison, the main national penitentiary in the Philippines, is located in:

- [ ] Quezon City
- [x] Muntinlupa City
- [ ] Davao City
- [ ] Zamboanga City

> Explanation: The New Bilibid Prison, managed by the Bureau of Corrections, is the central correctional facility located in Muntinlupa City, Metro Manila.

### An act of grace from the President which exempts an individual from the punishment the law inflicts for a crime they have committed is called:

- [x] Pardon
- [ ] Amnesty
- [ ] Probation
- [ ] Parole

> Explanation: A pardon is a form of executive clemency that completely absolves an individual from the legal consequences of their crime. A plenary pardon extinguishes all criminal liability, while a conditional pardon requires the fulfillment of certain conditions.

### What is the primary purpose of the "Therapeutic Community Modality Program" widely used in Philippine jails and prisons?

- [ ] To provide vocational training.
- [ ] To enforce strict discipline through punishment.
- [x] To treat and rehabilitate individuals with substance abuse problems and behavioral issues through a peer-led community environment.
- [ ] To separate troublesome inmates from the general population.

> Explanation: The Therapeutic Community (TC) is a self-help social learning treatment model where participants, with the help of staff and peers, work to overcome their issues and develop a more pro-social lifestyle.

### This refers to the reduction of the duration of a prison sentence by the President.

- [ ] Pardon
- [x] Commutation of Sentence
- [ ] Reprieve
- [ ] Amnesty

> Explanation: Commutation of sentence is a form of executive clemency that reduces a sentence to a lighter one (e.g., from death to life imprisonment, or from a longer term to a shorter term).

### Which of the following offenders is generally disqualified from being granted probation?

- [ ] A first-time offender.
- [x] One who has been convicted of a crime punishable by more than 6 years of imprisonment.
- [ ] An offender who is over 60 years old.
- [ ] An offender who has pleaded guilty.

> Explanation: Presidential Decree 968 (The Probation Law of 1976), as amended, disqualifies those sentenced to serve a maximum term of imprisonment of more than six years.

### The agency that manages provincial jails is the:

- [ ] BJMP
- [ ] BuCor
- [x] The respective Provincial Government
- [ ] The Philippine National Police

> Explanation: While city, district, and municipal jails are under the BJMP, provincial jails are administered and funded by the respective provincial governments.

### The system where prisoners are confined in their cells day and night is known as the:

- [ ] Auburn System
- [x] Pennsylvania System
- [ ] Congregate System
- [ ] Elmira System

> Explanation: The Pennsylvania System, or "separate system," emphasized solitary confinement at all times to encourage reflection and penitence, preventing inmates from corrupting one another.

### This system, also known as the "congregate system," allowed inmates to work together during the day in silence but were confined to individual cells at night.

- [x] Auburn System
- [ ] Pennsylvania System
- [ ] Borstal System
- [ ] Irish System

> Explanation: The Auburn System (or New York System) was a competitor to the Pennsylvania model and became the more widely adopted system due to its economic advantages of inmate labor.

### The Iwahig Penal Colony in Palawan is an example of what type of correctional facility?

- [x] Open Penal Institution / Penal Farm
- [ ] Maximum Security Prison
- [ ] City Jail
- [ ] Supermax Facility

> Explanation: Iwahig is a penal farm under the Bureau of Corrections, representing an "open" system where inmates have more freedom of movement and are engaged in agricultural and other productive activities.

### The period of deducted sentence granted to a prisoner for good conduct and diligence is called:

- [ ] Parole
- [x] Good Conduct Time Allowance (GCTA)
- [ ] Commutation
- [ ] Reprieve

> Explanation: GCTA is a privilege granted to inmates under the law (RA 10592) which allows for a reduction of their sentence for demonstrating good behavior and participating in rehabilitation programs.

### What is the primary function of the Board of Pardons and Parole (BPP)?

- [ ] To manage the daily operations of prisons.
- [ ] To prosecute parole violators.
- [x] To grant parole and recommend pardon and other forms of executive clemency to the President.
- [ ] To supervise probationers.

> Explanation: The BPP is the administrative body responsible for deciding which inmates are eligible for parole and for recommending to the President who should receive executive clemency.

### A general pardon extended to a group of persons, often for political offenses, is called:

- [ ] Pardon
- [x] Amnesty
- [ ] Reprieve
- [ ] Commutation

> Explanation: Amnesty is a sovereign act of oblivion for past acts, granted by a government to a group of persons. It requires the concurrence of a majority of all the members of the Congress.

### The postponement of the execution of a sentence to a certain date is known as a:

- [ ] Commutation
- [x] Reprieve
- [ ] Pardon
- [ ] Amnesty

> Explanation: A reprieve is a temporary stay of the execution of a sentence, often used in death penalty cases to allow for further review.

### Which correctional model focuses on changing the offender's behavior through treatment and rehabilitation programs?

- [ ] Custodial Model
- [x] Rehabilitation Model
- [ ] Reintegration Model
- [ ] Justice Model

> Explanation: The Rehabilitation Model views crime as a result of underlying social or psychological problems that can be "cured" through various therapies, education, and vocational training.

### What is the purpose of classifying inmates upon their admission to a correctional facility?

- [ ] To assign them their prison jobs.
- [x] To determine their security risk level and program needs.
- [ ] To get their personal information for the record.
- [ ] To introduce them to the prison staff.

> Explanation: The classification process involves assessing the inmate's background, criminal history, and personal characteristics to place them in the appropriate security level (maximum, medium, minimum) and to identify suitable rehabilitation programs.

### This branch of penology deals with the management and administration of correctional institutions like jails and prisons.

- [x] Institutional Corrections
- [ ] Community-Based Corrections
- [ ] Penology
- [ ] Victimology

> Explanation: Institutional corrections refers to the aspect of the correctional enterprise that involves the incarceration and rehabilitation of adults and juveniles convicted of offenses against the law.

### The concept of parole in the Philippines is based on which historical system?

- [ ] The English Borstal Institution
- [x] The Irish System developed by Sir Walter Crofton
- [ ] The Elmira Reformatory in New York
- [ ] The Auburn Prison System

> Explanation: The Irish System was a progressive stage system that rewarded good conduct with increased privileges and culminated in a "ticket of leave," which was a conditional release that is the direct forerunner of modern parole.

### What is the legal basis of the Parole and Probation Administration in the Philippines?

- [x] Presidential Decree No. 968
- [ ] Republic Act No. 6975
- [ ] Republic Act No. 10592
- [ ] Executive Order No. 292

> Explanation: Presidential Decree No. 968, signed in 1976, is "The Probation Law of 1976," which established the probation system and created the Probation Administration (later renamed Parole and Probation Administration).

### The "halfway house" is a feature of which correctional model?

- [ ] Custodial Model
- [ ] Rehabilitation Model
- [x] Reintegration Model
- [ ] Justice Model

> Explanation: The Reintegration Model emphasizes the importance of preparing offenders to return to the community. Halfway houses are transitional facilities that provide structure and support to offenders just before or after their release from prison.

### Who is considered the "Father of Probation" in the Philippines?

- [x] Teodulo C. Natividad
- [ ] John Augustus
- [ ] Manuel A. Roxas
- [ ] Ferdinand E. Marcos

> Explanation: Congressman Teodulo C. Natividad was the primary author and sponsor of the bill that became the Probation Law of 1976 (PD 968). He is widely recognized as the Father of Probation in the Philippines.

### The highest security level in a Philippine prison, housing the most dangerous and high-risk inmates, is:

- [x] Maximum Security Compound
- [ ] Medium Security Compound
- [ ] Minimum Security Compound
- [ ] Supermax Facility

> Explanation: The Maximum Security Compound houses inmates who are considered highly dangerous, high-security risks, or those serving life sentences or sentenced to death (though the death penalty is currently abolished).

### This refers to the process of releasing an inmate from prison before the completion of their maximum sentence.

- [ ] Graduation
- [x] Parole
- [ ] Expiration of sentence
- [ ] Pardon

> Explanation: Parole is the conditional release from prison that allows an individual to serve the remainder of their sentence in the community under supervision.
