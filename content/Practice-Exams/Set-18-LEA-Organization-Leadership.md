### 1. Which principle of management states that every subordinate should report to one and only one superior?

- [ ] Span of Control
- [x] Unity of Command
- [ ] Chain of Command
- [ ] Division of Work

> Explanation: Unity of Command is a core organizational principle ensuring that each subordinate is accountable to only one supervisor, which prevents conflicting orders and confusion. Span of Control refers to the number of subordinates a supervisor can effectively manage.

### 2. Under RA 8551, the PNP is described as a "community and service oriented agency." What is its character?

- [ ] Military in character, national in scope.
- [x] Civilian in character, national in scope.
- [ ] National in character, civilian in scope.
- [ ] Military in character, local in scope.

> Explanation: Section 2 of RA 8551, the "PNP Reform and Reorganization Act of 1998," explicitly states that the PNP shall be a community and service-oriented agency which is civilian in character and national in scope.

### 3. Which of the following is a primary line function of a police organization?

- [ ] Planning and Research
- [x] Patrol Operations
- [ ] Personnel and Training
- [ ] Budget and Finance

> Explanation: Line functions are those that directly carry out the major purposes of the organization. Patrol operations are the backbone of police work, directly serving the public and preventing crime. Planning, personnel, and finance are considered staff or administrative functions that support the line units.

### 4. What does the 'D' in the POSDCORB management acronym stand for?

- [ ] Delegating
- [x] Directing
- [ ] Developing
- [ ] Dispatching

> Explanation: POSDCORB is an acronym for Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, Coordinating, Reporting, and Budgeting. Directing involves the continuous task of making decisions, embodying them in specific and general orders, and serving as the leader of the enterprise.

### 5. The National Police Commission (NAPOLCOM) exercises administrative control and operational supervision over the PNP. Who serves as its ex-officio Chairperson?

- [ ] The Chief of the PNP.
- [x] The Secretary of the Interior and Local Government (DILG).
- [ ] A civilian appointed by the President.
- [ ] The National Security Advisor.

> Explanation: Under RA 8551, the Secretary of the DILG is the ex-officio Chairperson of the NAPOLCOM, ensuring civilian authority over the police force.

### 6. A police lieutenant has 12 patrol officers directly under her command. This number relates to which organizational concept?

- [ ] Unity of Command
- [x] Span of Control
- [ ] Delegation of Authority
- [ ] Chain of Command

> Explanation: Span of Control refers to the optimal number of subordinates a manager can effectively supervise. A span of 3 to 7 is often considered ideal, but this can vary based on the complexity of the tasks.

### 7. Which leadership theory suggests that leaders are born, not made, and possess certain inherent characteristics?

- [ ] Behavioral Theory
- [x] Trait Theory
- [ ] Situational Theory
- [ ] Path-Goal Theory

> Explanation: The Trait Theory of leadership is based on the assumption that leaders have specific innate traits (e.g., intelligence, charisma, determination) that differentiate them from non-leaders.

### 8. The power of a local chief executive to exercise operational supervision and control over PNP units in their jurisdiction is known as:

- [ ] Power of Command
- [x] Deputation by NAPOLCOM
- [ ] Administrative Authority
- [ ] Plenary Power

> Explanation: Governors and Mayors, upon being deputized by the NAPOLCOM, can exercise operational supervision and control over PNP units within their respective jurisdictions. This is a key feature of local police integration.

### 9. What is the highest non-commissioned officer rank in the Philippine National Police?

- [ ] Police Corporal
- [ ] Police Staff Sergeant
- [x] Police Executive Master Sergeant
- [ ] Police Chief Master Sergeant

> Explanation: Based on the rank classification under RA 11200, Police Executive Master Sergeant (PEMS) is the highest "Police Non-Commissioned Officer" (PNCO) rank before entering the commissioned officer ranks.

### 10. This management function involves grouping activities, assigning personnel, and delegating authority to achieve organizational goals.

- [ ] Planning
- [x] Organizing
- [ ] Staffing
- [ ] Coordinating

> Explanation: Organizing is the process of creating the structure of the organization. It involves determining what tasks are to be done, who is to do them, how the tasks are to be grouped, who reports to whom, and where decisions are to be made.

### 11. Which law established the Philippine National Police (PNP) under a reorganized Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG)?

- [ ] RA 8551
- [x] RA 6975
- [ ] RA 9708
- [ ] RA 11200

> Explanation: RA 6975, the "DILG Act of 1990," created the PNP by merging the former Philippine Constabulary and the Integrated National Police. It placed this new police force under the administration of the DILG.

### 12. A police leader who adjusts their style based on the readiness and willingness of their subordinates is applying which theory?

- [ ] Trait Theory
- [ ] Fiedler's Contingency Model
- [x] Hersey and Blanchard's Situational Leadership Theory
- [ ] Autocratic Leadership

> Explanation: The Situational Leadership Theory by Hersey and Blanchard posits that effective leadership depends on matching the leader's style (telling, selling, participating, delegating) to the maturity or development level of the followers.

### 13. Which of the following is a key function of NAPOLCOM?

- [ ] Appointing the Chief of the PNP.
- [x] Administering police entrance examinations.
- [ ] Prosecuting erring police officers.
- [ ] Formulating the annual PNP budget.

> Explanation: NAPOLCOM is tasked with administering the recruitment and selection process for the PNP, which includes conducting the police entrance examinations to ensure that applicants meet the minimum qualifications.

### 14. In the PNP's promotion system, "time-in-grade" refers to:

- [ ] The total years of service in the PNP.
- [x] The minimum time spent in a particular rank before being eligible for the next higher rank.
- [ ] The time elapsed since the last disciplinary action.
- [ ] The duration of a police officer's training.

> Explanation: Time-in-Grade (TIG) is a fundamental requirement for promotion that ensures an officer has sufficient experience at their current rank before being considered for advancement.

### 15. The principle of "line and staff" in a police organization is designed to:

- [ ] Create a system of checks and balances.
- [x] Provide support and specialized expertise to the operational units.
- [ ] Establish a clear hierarchy of command.
- [ ] Give all officers equal authority.

> Explanation: The staff component exists to advise, support, and provide specialized services (like forensics, training, legal advice) to the line units, which are responsible for the primary law enforcement duties. This allows line officers to focus on their core mission.

### 16. What is the minimum educational qualification for appointment to the PNP as a Patrolman/Patrolwoman?

- [ ] A high school diploma.
- [x] A baccalaureate degree.
- [ ] A master's degree.
- [ ] A Criminology graduate.

> Explanation: As part of the professionalization of the police service under RA 8551, a baccalaureate degree from a recognized institution of learning is the minimum educational requirement for entry into the PNP.

### 17. The concept that an organization should have a formal structure where rules are applied impersonally is a key tenet of:

- [ ] Scientific Management
- [x] Bureaucratic Management
- [ ] Human Relations Theory
- [ ] Systems Theory

> Explanation: Max Weber's theory of Bureaucracy emphasizes a formal hierarchy, a clear division of labor, and the impartial application of rules and procedures to ensure efficiency and fairness.

### 18. The relationship between the DILG and the PNP is one of:

- [ ] Subordination and control.
- [x] Administrative supervision and coordination.
- [ ] Direct command and operational control.
- [ ] Partnership and collaboration only.

> Explanation: The DILG, through NAPOLCOM, exercises administrative supervision over the PNP. This means it oversees policy, administration, and professionalization, while operational command remains with the PNP's own chain of command.

### 19. A police captain who consults with his subordinates and encourages their participation in decision-making is exhibiting what leadership style?

- [ ] Autocratic
- [ ] Laissez-faire
- [x] Democratic
- [ ] Transactional

> Explanation: A democratic (or participative) leader involves team members in the decision-making process. This style encourages collaboration and can lead to higher job satisfaction and creativity.

### 20. The initial integration of local police forces into a unified national police force was mandated by:

- [ ] The 1987 Constitution
- [x] Presidential Decree No. 765
- [ ] Republic Act 4864
- [ ] Republic Act 6975

> Explanation: PD 765, issued in 1975, ordered the constitution of the Integrated National Police (INP), which brought together the local police forces with the Philippine Constabulary as its nucleus. This was a precursor to the modern PNP.

### 21. Which function of management involves monitoring performance, comparing it with goals, and correcting any significant deviations?

- [ ] Planning
- [ ] Leading
- [x] Controlling
- [ ] Organizing

> Explanation: Controlling is the management function that ensures that the organization's activities are proceeding as planned. It involves setting standards, measuring actual performance, and taking corrective action.

### 22. Which PNP National Support Unit is the primary investigation arm of the police?

- [ ] Special Action Force (SAF)
- [x] Criminal Investigation and Detection Group (CIDG)
- [ ] Highway Patrol Group (HPG)
- [ ] Intelligence Group (IG)

> Explanation: The CIDG is the primary PNP unit mandated to undertake the investigation, detection, and prosecution of major crimes such as organized crime, economic crimes, and other heinous offenses.

### 23. This principle suggests that authority should flow from the top down to the bottom of the organization in a clear and unbroken line.

- [ ] Span of Control
- [ ] Unity of Command
- [x] Scalar Principle (Chain of Command)
- [ ] Delegation of Authority

> Explanation: The Scalar Principle, also known as the Chain of Command, establishes a clear line of authority, ensuring that every person in the organization knows who they report to and who reports to them.

### 24. What is the mandatory retirement age for officers and non-officers of the PNP?

- [ ] 55
- [x] 56
- [ ] 60
- [ ] 65

> Explanation: Under existing laws, the mandatory retirement age for all members of the PNP is fifty-six (56) years old.

### 25. A leadership theory that focuses on the leader's ability to inspire and motivate followers to exceed their expected performance is called:

- [ ] Transactional Leadership
- [x] Transformational Leadership
- [ ] Servant Leadership
- [ ] Situational Leadership

> Explanation: Transformational leadership involves leaders who inspire and motivate their followers to achieve extraordinary outcomes and, in the process, develop their own leadership capacity. They create a vision and act as role models.

### 26. Which of the following is NOT a basis for waiver of the minimum qualifications for appointment to the PNP?

- [ ] Age
- [ ] Weight
- [x] Educational Attainment
- [ ] Height

> Explanation: While waivers for age, weight, and height may be granted by NAPOLCOM under certain conditions, the minimum educational requirement of a baccalaureate degree cannot be waived.

### 27. In the context of police management, "staffing" primarily involves:

- [ ] Assigning patrol routes.
- [x] Recruiting, selecting, training, and developing personnel.
- [ ] Procuring equipment and supplies.
- [ ] Creating the organizational chart.

> Explanation: Staffing is the human resource function of management. It is concerned with finding the right people for the right jobs, orienting them, training them, and ensuring their continued development.

### 28. The power to summarily dismiss a PNP member for serious offenses is vested in the:

- [ ] Local Chief Executive
- [x] Chief of the PNP and Regional Directors
- [ ] President of the Philippines
- [ ] PLEB

> Explanation: The Chief of the PNP and Regional Directors have the authority to summarily dismiss any PNP member when the charge is serious and the evidence of guilt is strong, among other grounds specified in RA 8551.

### 29. A police organization that is highly structured, with rigid rules and a clear chain of command, is often referred to as:

- [ ] An organic system
- [x] A mechanistic system
- [ ] A matrix organization
- [ ] A flat organization

> Explanation: A mechanistic structure is characterized by high specialization, formalization, and centralization. It is rigid and bureaucratic, similar to a traditional military or police organization, designed for efficiency and control.

### 30. Which body is empowered to hear and decide citizen's complaints formally filed against any member of the PNP?

- [ ] The local court
- [ ] The Internal Affairs Service (IAS)
- [x] The People's Law Enforcement Board (PLEB)
- [ ] The local mayor's office

> Explanation: The PLEB is a local disciplinary body established by RA 6975 (and strengthened by RA 8551) to hear and decide citizen's complaints against uniformed members of the PNP. It is a primary feature of citizen oversight.
